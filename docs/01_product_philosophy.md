# 01 – Product Philosophy

**Project:** Valens  
**Version:** 0.2  
**Status:** Phase 1 specification  
**Last updated:** 2026-07-05

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the stable principles behind Valens. It should guide product, architecture and implementation decisions.

When there is conflict between a flashy feature and these principles, these principles win.

---

## 2. Capacity over performance

Most fitness applications reward performance:

- more weight
- more reps
- faster pace
- longer streaks
- higher calories
- more intensity

Valens rewards capacity:

- can the user move comfortably?
- can the user recover?
- can the user remain consistent?
- can the user tolerate daily life better?
- can the user keep recreational activities?

Valens may track performance, but performance is not the final objective. Performance is one signal among many.

---

## 3. Consistency over intensity

A moderate program followed for years is superior to an intense program abandoned in weeks.

The product should encourage:

- small daily actions
- flexible planning
- sustainable progress
- forgiving recovery
- easy restart after interruptions

It should avoid:

- guilt-based streaks
- shame messages
- “no excuses” rhetoric
- maximal effort as a default

---

## 4. Pain is information

Pain is not ignored.

Pain is also not automatically treated as failure.

Valens separates:

- muscular effort
- muscular fatigue
- joint discomfort
- sharp pain
- instability
- fear or lack of confidence

Pain affects planning, progression and exercise selection.

A knee-sensitive user may still train squat patterns, but the planner must control dose, depth, variation, frequency and recovery.

---

## 5. Adaptation over prescription

There is no single ideal routine.

The right session depends on:

- recent training
- external activity
- sleep/recovery
- joint state
- available time
- progression history
- preferences
- movement coverage

Valens should behave less like a static program and more like a coach choosing the appropriate dose today.

---

## 6. Train for life

Every exercise should connect to functional meaning.

Examples:

- glute bridge supports hip extension and knee mechanics
- wall sit supports standing and stair tolerance
- single-leg balance supports fall prevention
- loaded mobility supports usable range
- upper pulling supports shoulder balance and posture
- carries support grip, trunk and real-world load

Valens should prefer exercises with clear functional transfer.

---

## 7. Respect existing activities

Users do not live inside the app.

Activities such as basketball, walking, hiking, cycling, gardening, swimming or manual work should be logged as external activities.

These activities contribute to training load and capacity. The planner should consider them rather than assuming the app session is the only effort.

---

## 8. Teach, do not merely time

Valens is a coach, not only a timer.

It should explain:

- why an exercise matters
- what movement pattern it trains
- which muscles are involved
- which joints are stressed
- common mistakes
- regressions
- alternatives
- progression logic

The user should become more movement-literate over time.

---

## 9. Long-term maintenance is success

Eventually, some capacities should plateau. That is not failure.

For healthy aging, maintaining a high level can be an excellent outcome.

Valens should support three states:

- building
- maintaining
- recovering

A mature user profile may maintain many capacities while focusing improvement on weak or declining areas.

---

## 10. Privacy and autonomy

Valens should work without:

- account creation
- cloud sync
- ads
- subscriptions
- social feeds
- unnecessary permissions

The user owns their data.

Export should be available in open formats.

---

## 11. Scientific humility

Valens should avoid exaggerated claims.

It should not promise reversal of aging, guaranteed pain relief, or medical outcomes.

When recommendations are heuristic, they should be transparent.

The app should remain compatible with professional medical advice and encourage consultation for serious or persistent symptoms.

---

## 12. Product manifesto

Valens exists because movement is a long-term investment.

The goal is not to create the hardest workout.

The goal is to help users accumulate thousands of small, appropriate training decisions that preserve independence, comfort and confidence.

Every feature should answer:

> Does this help the user move well not just today, but twenty years from now?
