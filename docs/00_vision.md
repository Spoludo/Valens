# 00 – Vision

**Project:** Valens  
**Version:** 0.2  
**Status:** Phase 1 specification  
**Last updated:** 2026-07-05

---

## 1. Mission

Valens helps people preserve and improve physical capacity throughout aging.

Its core mission is:

> Help users remain capable, independent, mobile and confident for as many years as possible.

The app is designed around healthy aging rather than maximal athletic performance. It supports strength, mobility, balance, joint resilience and functional endurance through adaptive short sessions that respond to pain, recovery and real-life activity.

---

## 2. What Valens is

Valens is:

- an adaptive movement coach
- a privacy-first Android app
- an offline-first training platform
- a data-driven exercise library
- a joint-aware planner
- a longevity dashboard
- a tool for personal physical capacity

Valens is not primarily:

- a weight-loss app
- a calorie tracker
- a bodybuilding app
- a social fitness network
- a competition platform
- a generic timer

---

## 3. Primary user promise

Valens should help the user answer:

> What should I train today so that I can keep moving well for years?

This requires more than a workout list. It requires a model of:

- what the user has done recently
- which movement patterns are undertrained
- which joints are sensitive
- which exercises are progressing
- which exercises cause pain
- which capacities are stable, improving or declining
- which external activities already provide useful load

---

## 4. Long-term success

Valens succeeds if users report that they can continue doing meaningful activities with less fear and more confidence.

Examples:

- shooting basketball casually
- walking long distances
- hiking
- travelling
- climbing stairs
- carrying groceries
- getting up from the floor
- playing with children or grandchildren
- maintaining posture
- avoiding persistent joint aggravation

The long-term measure is not “maximum wall sit time” but whether the user preserves usable physical freedom.

---

## 5. Core pillars

Valens organizes training around five pillars.

### Strength

The ability to generate and tolerate force.

Examples:

- pushing
- pulling
- carrying
- standing
- climbing
- bracing

### Mobility

The ability to use joint range of motion with control.

Mobility is not passive flexibility alone. It is functional range supported by strength.

### Balance

The ability to remain stable during static and dynamic tasks.

Balance includes proprioception, unilateral control and confidence under load.

### Joint resilience

The ability of joints and connective tissues to tolerate repeated life demands without recurring pain.

This requires appropriate loading, recovery and gradual progression.

### Functional endurance

The ability to repeat ordinary tasks without excessive fatigue.

Valens does not replace walking, cycling, basketball or hiking. It complements them.

---

## 6. Default routine philosophy

The default routine target is approximately 20 minutes.

This is long enough to produce meaningful training and short enough to remain sustainable.

The 20 minutes include:

- preparation
- exercise time
- rest periods
- transitions
- brief feedback collection

Progression should not automatically lengthen the daily routine indefinitely. If exercises become longer or harder, the planner may reduce set count, rotate patterns, or move an exercise to maintenance mode.

---

## 7. Open-source platform vision

Valens should eventually support community exercise packs.

Examples:

- Isometric Foundations
- Mobility Foundations
- Balance for Aging
- Knee-Friendly Strength
- Basketball Support
- Resistance Bands
- Tai Chi Inspired Mobility
- Yoga Mobility
- Posture and Desk Worker Pack

The planner should reason from metadata rather than hardcoded exercise names, so new packs can integrate naturally.

---

## 8. Product north star

Every major decision should be evaluated against this question:

> Will this help people remain physically capable and independent for longer?

If the answer is no, the feature should be reconsidered.
