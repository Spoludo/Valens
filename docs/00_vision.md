**Project:** Longevity Coach

**Version:** 0.1

**Status:** Draft

**Last updated:** 2026-07-05

# 1. Vision

Longevity Coach is an open-source Android application whose objective is to help people maintain and improve their physical capacity throughout aging.

Unlike traditional fitness applications, the project does not optimize for athletic performance, bodybuilding, calorie expenditure, or competitive achievement.

Instead, it optimizes for one objective:

> **Maximize healthy physical independence over the lifespan.**

The application should help users remain capable of performing everyday activities with confidence, comfort and minimal pain for as many years as possible.

Examples include:

- climbing stairs
- getting up from the floor
- carrying groceries
- hiking
- recreational sports
- travelling
- playing with children or grandchildren
- maintaining posture
- reducing chronic joint pain
- preventing falls

---

# 2. Philosophy

The project follows several principles.

## Capacity over Performance

The objective is not to become the strongest possible person.

The objective is to maintain enough reserve capacity that normal daily activities remain easy throughout life.

---

## Sustainability over Intensity

The best program is the one that can realistically be followed for decades.

Short, consistent sessions are preferred over demanding workouts that increase dropout rates.

Default target:

* approximately 20 minutes per day

---

## Adaptation over Prescription

No fixed training plan is ideal for everyone.

Training should adapt continuously according to:

- recovery
- pain
- previous workload
- available time
- progression
- recreational activities
- user preferences

---

## Pain-aware Training

Pain is information.

Pain should never simply cause progression to stop.

Instead it becomes one of the planner's inputs.

The planner learns which exercises produce discomfort and gradually proposes alternatives while maintaining training quality.

---

## Privacy First

The application should work entirely offline.

No account should be required.

No cloud synchronization is required.

Users own their own data.

---

## Open Platform

Exercises should not be hardcoded.

New exercise packs should be installable without modifying the application source code.

Examples:

- Isometric Pack
- Mobility Pack
- Yoga Pack
- Tai Chi Pack
- Knee Rehabilitation Pack
- Basketball Pack

---

# 3. Target Users

Primary audience:

Adults aged approximately 40–80 who wish to preserve physical function.

Secondary audiences:

- rehabilitation patients (under professional guidance)
- recreational athletes
- office workers
- retirees
- beginners
- developers interested in exercise science

The application is **not** intended primarily for:

- bodybuilding
- powerlifting
- CrossFit programming
- competitive athletic coaching

---

# 4. Core Objectives

The application attempts to improve five long-term capacities.

## Strength

Ability to generate force.

Examples:

- pushing
- carrying
- standing up
- climbing

---

## Mobility

Maintain usable joint range of motion.

Not flexibility for its own sake.

Mobility should improve functional movement.

---

## Balance

Improve proprioception and reduce fall risk.

Includes:

- static balance

- dynamic balance

- unilateral stability

---

## Joint Resilience

Maintain healthy joints by balancing loading and recovery.

The planner should avoid repeatedly stressing sensitive joints while still encouraging gradual adaptation.

---

## Functional Endurance

Improve the ability to perform ordinary daily activities without fatigue.

Examples:

- walking

- gardening

- hiking

- recreational basketball

---

# 5. Product Principles

Every feature should support at least one of the following.

✓ Better long-term health

✓ Better adherence

✓ Better movement quality

✓ Better understanding of one's body

✓ Reduced injury risk

✓ Increased motivation

Features that only increase complexity without improving one of these principles should generally be rejected.

---

# 6. What Success Looks Like

The application succeeds if, years later, users report:

"I still enjoy doing the things I love."

rather than

"I reached Level 99."

Long-term health is considered more important than continuous numerical progression.

---

# 7. Example User

Age: 55

Works mostly seated.

Exercises at home.

Plays basketball recreationally.

Has previous knee surgery.

Wants to remain active into old age.

Prefers 20-minute daily sessions.

The planner automatically adjusts sessions according to recovery, pain, previous activity, and long-term capacity goals.

---

# 8. Non-goals

The project deliberately avoids becoming:

- a calorie counter
- a bodybuilding application
- a social network
- a weight-loss application
- an activity tracker
- a wearable-first platform

Those problems are already solved elsewhere.

Longevity Coach focuses on preserving and improving physical capacity.

---

# 9. Definition of Success

A successful workout is **not** the hardest workout.

A successful workout is one that:

- is completed
- improves or maintains capacity
- respects pain and recovery
- contributes to long-term consistency
- leaves the user better prepared for tomorrow

Consistency is considered the primary driver of long-term success.

---

# 10. Long-term Vision

The application should evolve into an extensible platform capable of supporting:

- adaptive planning
- intelligent progression
- community exercise packs
- rehabilitation protocols
- movement assessments
- AI-assisted coaching
- wearable integration

while remaining open-source, privacy-respecting, and scientifically grounded.

The guiding question behind every design decision should be:

> **"Will this help people remain physically capable and independent for longer?"**
